ReEvolution Case Study

A website redesign with ReEvolution — an incarceration-focused nonprofit based in Los Angeles — aimed to enhance user experience and better communicate the organization's mission, impact, and opportunities for engagement.

Who & what

Role: Lead UI/UX Designer, along with fellow Lead UI/UX Designer Tayler Byrne.

Responsibilities: Conducted user research, led the design ideation and prototyping phases, coordinated user testing, and synthesized feedback for iterative design improvements.

Length: 2 Weeks

Research & Definition

To accurately and intentionally redesign ReEvolution’s website, we needed to understand more about the organization and its users (or its potential users).

Here’s how we gathered intel to inform our definitions.

We asked more than 50 respondents what they seek from a nonprofit organization. (Because we didn’t have immediate access to ReEvolution staff or volunteers, we were forced to broaden our initial research.)

We interviewed Barbara Van Sickle, the founder of ReEvolution.

We conducted a heuristic evaluation on ReEvolution’s website and researched similar organizations’ digital presences.

Here's What We Learned

Potential volunteers and donors are drawn to causes that personally resonate with them, valuing the nonprofit's reputation and impact, and emphasizing the importance of detailed program descriptions, financial transparency, and a seamless process for volunteering and donating on the nonprofit’s website.

AND...

ReEvolution experiences challenges in funding, communication, and community maintenance.
