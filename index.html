ReEvolution Case Study

A website redesign with ReEvolution — an incarceration-focused nonprofit based in Los Angeles — aimed to enhance user experience and better communicate the organization's mission, impact, and opportunities for engagement.

Who & what

Role: Lead UI/UX Designer, along with fellow Lead UI/UX Designer Tayler Byrne.

Responsibilities: Conducted user research, led the design ideation and prototyping phases, coordinated user testing, and synthesized feedback for iterative design improvements.

Length: 2 Weeks

Research & Definition

To accurately and intentionally redesign ReEvolution’s website, we needed to understand more about the organization and its users (or its potential users).

Here’s how we gathered intel to inform our definitions.

We asked more than 50 respondents what they seek from a nonprofit organization. (Because we didn’t have immediate access to ReEvolution staff or volunteers, we were forced to broaden our initial research.)

We interviewed Barbara Van Sickle, the founder of ReEvolution.

We conducted a heuristic evaluation on ReEvolution’s website and researched similar organizations’ digital presences.

Here's What We Learned

Potential volunteers and donors are drawn to causes that personally resonate with them, valuing the nonprofit's reputation and impact, and emphasizing the importance of detailed program descriptions, financial transparency, and a seamless process for volunteering and donating on the nonprofit’s website.

AND...

ReEvolution experiences challenges in funding, communication, and community maintenance.

Ideation & Brainstorm

How might we create a solution that addresses what volunteers and donors want and the challenges ReEvolution faces?

We started by imagining one of ReEvolution’s most important users — the donor and the volunteer, Isabella.

Isabella’s pain points, goals, needs — everything listed above — influenced every decision we make. How might we Isabella think about this or that design decision?

Next, we positioned Isabella’s current and proposed user journeys together to identify where we could make the most impact.

  • Isabella’s Current Journey
  • 1. Has time/money and needs an organization she feels motivated to align herself with.
  • 2. Hears about ReEvolution but isn’t sure how it differs/compares to other organizations in her area.
  • 3. She goes to its website but still isn’t sure if her values align. Is she willing to spend money and/or time with them?
  • 4. To her, the organization appears disorganized — it lacks information and isn’t very transparent.
  • 5. Isabella sends a volunteer inquiry ... but she’s not expecting a response. She continues her journey elsewhere.
  • Isabella’s Proposed Journey
  • 1. Has time/money and needs an organization she feels motivated to align herself with.
  • 2. Hears about ReEvolution but isn’t sure how it differs/compares to other organizations in her area.
  • 3. She’s drawn to the work the organization is doing and the mission that is driving them.
  • 4. Isabella is pleased to learn that she has the opportunity to assist by volunteering.
  • 5. Her positive interaction causes her to spread the word of ReEvolution’s work, leading to organizational benefits & growth.

Based on our user’s journeys, we brainstormed ways to make their interaction with ReEvolution better. The three highlighted areas helped inform what focused on:

    - Donation Page
    - Newsletter Signup
    - Reports

DONATE PAGE

The Donate Page on the existing ReEvolution website was disorganized and lacked CTAs. We changed that.

"Our Impact" — using bold figures, brand-colored shapes, and simple copy — claerly highlights the role ReEvolution has in supporting current and formerly incarcerated peoples. At a glimpse, Isabella can see the impact ReEvolution has.

In the "Who We Support" section, we hear from the people who have benefited from the program — putting a “face to a name.” “I might be able to support people in a similar way,” Isabella thinks.

Lastly, in "Every Donation Helps," we brought to light the fiscal impact a donation can have on ReEvolution. “I want to donate,” Isabella thinks, “but where does my money go? This helps me imagine my impact instead of hoping for the best.”

Learnings & Next Steps

We learned much over this project — how small and midsize nonprofits work and what they struggle with, how users respond to information and ideas — but we came away with two overarching themes:

Clarity: Because there are many facets of ReEvolution, it’s important to ensure language around programs, opportunities to get involved, and ways to support the organization are clear. 

Accessibility: Information and user journeys need to consider all of the users of the website: people seeking services, volunteers, and donors.

As for what’s next, we’re excited about two areas:

Connecting: We would like to continue working with ReEvolution to continually refine our solution for them (and, potentially, for other nonprofits in their space).

Refining the Donation Page: This is arguably the most important part of ReEvolution’s website. How might we continue to test and refine it for more conversions?
